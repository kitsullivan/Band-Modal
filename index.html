Andicus

Andicus is the music of Columbus Ohio native, Andy Kuss. Starting with folk music as an 8 year old playing his grandfather's ukulele, Andicus quickly picked up the guitar at the age of 10 or so and was writing songs by the age of 12. Developing a style of playing along the way through a mix of learning styles ranging from primarily thousands of hours of listening and playing by ear, to private classical guitar lessons, Jazz guitar lessons and participation in Miami University's Jazz ensemble, and Steel Drum band. Along the way Andicus has participated in the creation of original Bluegrass music, Hip-Hop, Funk, Math-rock, Blues, R&B and most recently Pop music as he has learned to meld his various influences into a unified sound that projects a positive and uplifting message out into the world.

Hannah Hampton

Hannah Hampton is a Columbus local who spends her weekdays singing to her kindergarteners she teaches and her weekends singing to her friends (new and old) at restaurants and bars around town. Her original pop songs are all written about experiences she’s had in life. She also likes to cover music that people know and will sing along to with her. Whether that be Umbrella by Rihanna or Hotel California — she covers it all. Her favorite part about performing is the connections she gets to make with the strangers that are listening. Whether it be an actual conversation with someone or eye contact they make while they’re both singing along to the same song— Hannah truly believes music brings people together in a shared moment where everything else in life (our troubles, differences, etc.) can be pushed aside for a brief moment in time.

Topher James & the Biscuit Brigade

Suzuki Music Columbus Tour Group Orchestra

Following Shinichi Suzuki’s philosophy of Talent Education, we believe every child can learn and talent can be developed in every child when one creates the right environment. At Suzuki Music Columbus, we aspire to create a music learning community which embraces excellence and nurtures the human spirit.
"every child can learn and talent can be developed in every child"
Students in our Suzuki strings programs will learn a common repertoire of songs using the Suzuki Method. We teach children music in the way that they learn language -- first by ear and later by reading. In our music lessons, we use the “vocabulary” of a common repertoire to build fluency, confidence, and playing skills that will enhance their violin, viola, or cello playing.

Michael Cox

Dr. Michael Cox is a tenured Professor of Saxophone and Jazz Studies at Capital University where he has taught since 1990. While teaching full-time Michael maintains a free-lance schedule, recently playing midwest tours with Grammy-award winning jazz artist Diane Schuur while continuing to performing regularly with the Columbus Jazz Orchestra, the Columbus Symphony and diverse small groups including the Black Swamp Saxophone Quartet, Flippo the band, and the High Street Stompers Dixieland Band. Michael received a Bachelor’s degree (1983) and Outstanding Alumnus Award (2000) from Cameron University, a Master of Music Performance from Wichita State University (1986), and a Doctor of Arts from the University of Northern Colorado (1996). Waves de Aché

Waves de Aché is a Columbus-based band blending modern jazz harmony with rhythms rooted in Afro-Cuban Salsa music. Aché is a term from Santeria religion (founded in Cuba) that refers to a positive life force energy that is experienced more easily than explained. This idea is mirrored in the music inspired by this tradition; the listener wants to listen and take in the richness of the harmony and melody, but the power of the rhythms make it hard to sit still. Waves de Aché is led by percussionist Max Marsillo and bassist Will Strickler, both graduates of the Capital University Conservatory of Music. The band plays original compositions and songs by Latin jazz masters such as Paquito D’Rivera, Hilton Ruiz, Ed Simon and more. Completing the group’s line-up are some of Ohio’s finest musicians, Nate Parker on drums, Kevin O’Neill on saxophone & flute, Anthony Stanco on trumpet & flugelhorn and Ben Maloney on piano.

http://www.willstricklermusic.com/waves-de-ache

Shane Weisman

Shane Weisman is a versatile, up-and-coming folk/rock singer-songwriter and self-taught multi-instrumentalist. Shane hails from Bexley, Ohio, and is now based in Nashville, TN. Prior to Nashville, Shane spent some time at Berklee College of Music studying guitar and songwriting. During his short time at Berklee, he caught the eye of one of his songwriting professors, and after only 2 semesters, Shane was guided to leave school, move to New York City, and start performing his original material. In 2019, Shane released his debut single, “Hold on to Your Lover,” which was chosen to be on iHeart Radio's “Modern Singer-Songwriter” playlist. Since then, Shane has opened for artists such as Brothers Osborne, Brandy Clark, and more. For the time being, you can catch Shane playing all over Nashville at local music bars and writers rounds.
